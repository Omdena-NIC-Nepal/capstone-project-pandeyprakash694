Climate Trends in Nepal (1981-2019)

Summary

This report analyzes temperature and precipitation trends across various districts in Nepal from 1981 to 2019. The data reveals seasonal patterns, with Monsoon seasons showing significantly higher precipitation and temperatures peaking in Spring and Monsoon.

Yearly Temperature Trend

The line chart shows the average annual temperature across all districts, indicating a gradual warming trend.

Seasonal Precipitation by District

The bar chart illustrates average precipitation by season across districts, highlighting the dominance of the Monsoon season.

Temperature vs Precipitation

The scatter plot explores the relationship between temperature and precipitation, with distinct seasonal patterns.

Interesting Fact

Kanchanpur has an unusually high average precipitation of 20.49 mm in Winter, a typically dry season!

Conclusion

The analysis highlights a warming trend in Nepal, with significant precipitation during the Monsoon season. These insights can inform agricultural planning and disaster preparedness.
